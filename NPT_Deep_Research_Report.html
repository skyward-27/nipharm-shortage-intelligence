Executive Summary

The NPT platform sits at the intersection of three well-validated problem spaces: pharmaceutical shortage prediction (proven by TraceLink, Premier CognitiveRx, and iethico), commodity price intelligence (proven by Vesper and ChAI), and NHS open-data analytics (proven by OpenPrescribing). All three have been built. None have been combined for the UK generic drugs market in the way NPT proposes. The core architecture — a structured data lake fed by automated scrapers, a gradient-boosted prediction model, and a procurement decision layer — is well-tested across industries. The primary technical risk is not the ML model; it is the molecule identity problem and the quality of the training labels (price concession events), both of which are solvable using freely available NHSBSA data.

Section 01

Key Findings at a Glance

Finding 1 · Maturity
The Problem Is Solved in the US — Not in the UK
TraceLink predicts US drug shortages 90 days ahead at >80% accuracy. Premier CognitiveRx detects 76% of shortages 3 weeks before announcement. Neither operates in the UK NHS market. This is the moat.
Finding 2 · ML Model
Gradient Boosting Is the Industry-Validated Choice
XGBoost/LightGBM outperforms LSTM and ARIMA for pharmaceutical shortage prediction. The Pall et al. (2023) study achieved 69% accuracy on 4-class shortage prediction using XGBoost with pharmacy demand data.
Finding 3 · Data
The NHS Has Uniquely Rich Open Data — Nobody Has Used It for This
NHSBSA Drug Tariff + Concession records date back to 2014 and are freely downloadable. OpenPrescribing has proven Python tooling exists. No competitor has built a shortage prediction model on this UK-specific dataset.
Finding 4 · Commodity Analogy
The Vesper/ChAI Model Is Directly Applicable
Commodity intelligence platforms (Vesper, ChAI) achieve 95%+ directional accuracy for 30-day price forecasts by combining 4,000+ price series from >150 sources with AI. The data architecture is structurally identical to what NPT needs.
Finding 5 · Moat
The Outcome Database Is the Real Competitive Barrier
Every successful platform in this space (TraceLink, CognitiveRx, Vesper) builds its moat through proprietary outcome data — not model sophistication. Once NPT records 18 months of "recommendation made → outcome observed," no new entrant can replicate that.
Finding 6 · Risk
The Molecule Identity Problem Is Universal and Has a Known Solution
iethico's CTO cited identifier reconciliation as "often realistically impossible to perform manually." Every platform in this space solved it first. The solution is a Molecule Master table (BNF → SNOMED → product name → dm+d) built in Week 1.

Section 04

The ML Model: What the Research Says

What Model to Use

Across six peer-reviewed studies and four commercial platforms, the evidence converges on gradient boosting (XGBoost / LightGBM) as the optimal model for this problem for four reasons:

  • Small training set resilience: With ~200–400 UK price concession events as positive labels, LightGBM handles sparse data far better than LSTM or deep learning approaches, which require 10,000+ events for reliable training.
  • Feature interpretability: SHAP values on gradient-boosted trees provide procurement-readable reason codes ("Risk driven by: price at 5-year floor + 3 prior concessions in 12 months + single manufacturer").
  • Benchmark accuracy: Pall et al. (2023) achieved 69% accuracy on 4-class shortage prediction using XGBoost on pharmacy demand data — without access to the Drug Tariff floor signal that NPT has.
  • Industry standard: Both CognitiveRx (Premier) and early TraceLink models used ensemble tree methods before scaling to larger proprietary networks.

CTO Insight: Dimensionality Problem

The Pall et al. study achieved 69% accuracy with ~15 features on Canadian pharmacy data. With ~200–400 UK training events (price concession labels), a 57-feature model is severely underdetermined. The validated approach from both CognitiveRx and the academic literature is to cluster signals into 6–8 composite scores (Supply Stress Score, Demand Pressure Score, Market Structure Score, Price Stress Score) before feeding the model. This improves both accuracy and explainability.

Section 06

Lessons from Commodity Price Intelligence

The structural problem NPT is solving — predict when a commodity (generic drug) will become unprofitable to produce at the regulated price, causing supply to exit — is identical to the problem that commodity intelligence platforms solve for raw materials. The methods transfer directly.

What Commodity Platforms Do That Drug Platforms Don't (Yet)

  • Directional accuracy over precision: Vesper targets 95%+ directional accuracy for 30-day predictions — not exact price targets. For procurement decisions, direction is what matters: "price will go up, buy now" vs "price will fall, wait." NPT should adopt the same framing: risk score trend direction, not precise concession probability.
  • Optimal buying window identification: Commodity platforms identify not just "risk is high" but "the optimal procurement window is 3–6 weeks from now, before Q4 demand spike." NPT's equivalent is the financial calculator: "BUY FORWARD — order 4.5 additional weeks by 15th March."
  • Proprietary price benchmark: Vesper's Proprietary Price Index (VPI) — aggregated from buyer/seller data across the network — is their core defensible asset. NPT's equivalent is the Unprofitable Floor Index: a proprietary calculation of the price below which historical UK supply has always deteriorated. This should be branded and published monthly as a benchmark.
  • Analyst + AI hybrid delivery: Every successful commodity platform uses AI to surface signals but analysts to contextualise and communicate them. CognitiveRx uses a "specialised team of data scientists and pharmacy professionals." Vesper has "in-house analysts working alongside AI models." NPT's early stage advantage is that the founders are the domain experts — the AI augments, not replaces, their judgment.
Business Model Insight from ChAI

ChAI evolved from price forecasting to offering commodity price insurance — procurement teams could hedge against raw material price spikes. NPT's equivalent business model evolution: start with shortage alerts (Phase 1), evolve to procurement recommendations with financial value calculations (Phase 2), eventually offer procurement hedging or preferred supplier facilitation (Phase 3). This is the same S-curve that ChAI, Vesper, and Kpler have all climbed.

Section 07

Why Similar Platforms Have Failed: The Anti-Patterns

As importantly as studying successful platforms, the academic literature and industry post-mortems identify consistent failure modes for drug intelligence platforms. All are relevant to NPT.

01
Reactive, Not Predictive
99% of current pharmacy shortage tools detect shortages after they occur. The NHS community pharmacy survey found 99% of staff encountered supply issues weekly, yet no early warning tool existed. Tools that alert during a shortage are table stakes — the value is in predicting them 4–8 weeks before concession.
02
Ignoring the Molecule Identity Problem
iethico's CTO identified identifier reconciliation as the most underestimated engineering challenge. Platforms that skip this step silently corrupt every JOIN across data sources — conflating 500mg and 850mg Metformin, missing branded/generic equivalents, double-counting parallel imports.
03
Direction Without Quantity
The academic literature (Pall et al., Berkeley 2024) consistently finds that shortage prediction tools achieve adoption only when they include actionable quantity recommendations. "High risk" alerts without "order X weeks by Y date" are ignored by procurement teams. CognitiveRx's weekly digest with drug scoring + recommended actions outperformed pure alert systems.
04
Over-Feature Engineering
Studies show models with 50+ features trained on <500 shortage events overfit severely. The XGBoost studies achieving 69%+ accuracy used <20 features. The validated path is: start with 8–10 features, validate, add incrementally.
05
Treating External Data as Stable Infrastructure
Multiple healthcare analytics platforms (and the broader data warehouse literature) show that external data sources — NHS open data portals, government APIs — change schema, break URLs, and disappear without warning. TraceLink built on their own serialised product network precisely to avoid this. NPT must build schema-resilient ingestion from Day 1.
06
No Outcome Tracking
The single most consistent difference between platforms that achieved commercial scale (TraceLink, CognitiveRx) and those that stalled is systematic outcome tracking. Without recording "we predicted shortage for drug X in March; did it happen?" you cannot improve the model, cannot prove ROI to customers, and cannot build the proprietary dataset that becomes the moat.
Section 08

Areas of Consensus and Debate

What All Sources Agree On

  • Drug shortages are currently detected reactively across all markets — the gap for predictive tools is real and large
  • Gradient boosting (XGBoost/LightGBM) consistently outperforms deep learning for shortage prediction on small datasets
  • Historical shortage frequency is the single strongest predictor in every published model
  • Identifier normalisation (molecule master) must be solved before any multi-source data integration
  • The outcome dataset — tracking predictions against real outcomes — is the primary commercial moat for any platform in this space
  • Procurement teams need quantity and timing recommendations, not just risk flags

Areas of Genuine Debate

  • WaveData dependency vs NHSBSA reconstruction: WaveData's 24-year proprietary dataset provides the cleanest historical floor calculation, but it is expensive (>£80K/year estimated) and a single point of failure. Academic research suggests the NHSBSA Drug Tariff history (2014–present) provides sufficient data to reconstruct a floor proxy at ~70% accuracy. Whether 70% is good enough for launch is an open question.
  • Prediction horizon: TraceLink targets 90 days; CognitiveRx achieves 128-day average early detection. UK-specific models using NHSBSA data have not yet been validated. The CPE concession signal typically appears 4–6 weeks before supply crisis — suggesting a 6–8 week prediction window is achievable from free data alone.
  • B2B vs B2B2C model: Commodity platforms (Vesper, ChAI) sell to procurement teams directly. Healthcare platforms (TraceLink) sell to health systems. iethico targets individual pharmacies. For NPT, the optimal first customer is unclear — it is likely a buying group (e.g. NHS procurement consortium) rather than an individual pharmacy, given the financial complexity of quantity recommendations.
Section 09

Recommended Build Path: The Evidence-Based Sequence

Synthesising the research across all platforms and papers, the evidence-validated sequence for building NPT is:

Week 0 (Before Any Code): Validate the Thesis

Download NHSBSA Drug Tariff Part VIIIA (2014–2024). For every price concession event (2019–2024): was the molecule within 15% of its 5-year price floor in the 6 months prior? If the hit rate is >65%, the core thesis is empirically validated on real UK data and you have your first publication-quality result. This takes 2 days in Python and costs nothing. This is the single most important thing to do before Week 1.

Week 1: Molecule Master + Data Pipeline

Build the BNF → dm+d → product name normalisation table. Fork OpenPrescribing's NCSO concession import scripts (MIT license, GitHub). Set up n8n to automate 8 free data source imports on monthly schedule. This is the foundation every other component rests on — and the step iethico and every other platform confirms must come first.

Week 2: Feature Store + Baseline Model

Build the 8–10 composite feature scores (Supply Stress, Demand Pressure, Price Stress, Market Structure, Regulatory Activity). Train LightGBM on 2019–2024 price concession labels. Target: beat random baseline. Use SHAP to validate that the top 3 feature importances match your domain hypothesis (floor proximity, shortage history, manufacturer count).

Week 3: Financial Calculator + Dashboard

Build the procurement decision layer: given a risk score, calculate "order X weeks of stock by Y date, estimated saving £Z if concession materialises." Build Streamlit dashboard with weekly digest. This is the component that separates a research tool from a commercial product — and the step that most platforms build too late.

Week 4: Outcome Database + PoC Gaps

Deploy outcome tracking on Day 1 of live use — before you have any predictions to track. Record: drug, risk score, date predicted, recommended action, what the customer actually did, what happened. This database, after 6 months, is worth more than the model itself. Identify the WaveData vs NHSBSA accuracy gap and document it as the Phase 2 investment case.

Research Gaps

What Is Still Unknown

  • UK-specific model accuracy: No published study has trained a shortage prediction model specifically on NHSBSA Drug Tariff + concession data. The 69% XGBoost accuracy (Pall et al.) was on Canadian pharmacy data. The UK NHS price concession mechanism is more structured than the Canadian equivalent, which may produce better accuracy — but this must be empirically tested.
  • WaveData vs NHSBSA floor proxy gap: The accuracy delta between WaveData's proprietary 24-year dataset and the NHSBSA-reconstructed floor is unknown. Quantifying this gap is the most important single research question for the NPT PoC.
  • Optimal prediction window for UK generics: CPE concession signals lead shortages by ~4–6 weeks in practice. Whether the NHSBSA floor signal provides meaningful lead time beyond 6 weeks is untested.
  • Customer willingness to act on algorithmic recommendations: All platforms note that procurement teams trust human analysts more than pure algorithmic outputs. How much of CognitiveRx's 76% detection rate translates to actual procurement behaviour change is not published.
